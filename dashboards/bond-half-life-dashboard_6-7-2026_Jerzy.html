Bond Strength ↔ Half-Life Dashboard

For a unimolecular homolytic bond cleavage at temperature T, what bond dissociation energy gives a half-life of t? Uses Arrhenius: k = A·exp(−Eₐ/RT), with t₁/₂ = ln(2)/k.

Equations▾
Arrhenius rate law — empirical
k = A · exp( −Eₐ / RT )
First-order rate constant. A is treated as T-independent.
Eyring (transition-state theory) — mechanistic (TST)
k = (k_B T / h) · exp( ΔS‡ / R ) · exp( −ΔH‡ / RT )
From TST. The (k_B T / h) prefactor ≈ 10¹³ s⁻¹ at 300 K, T-dependent. ΔG‡ = ΔH‡ − TΔS‡.
Relationship between the two
Eₐ = ΔH‡ + RT   ·   A = (e · k_B T / h) · exp( ΔS‡ / R )
Identical when calibrated. ΔS‡ folds into A. RT term ≈ 0.6 kcal/mol at 300 K — usually within model uncertainty.
First-order half-life
t₁/₂ = ln(2) / k
Solved for bond strength — what this dashboard computes
Arrhenius:  Eₐ = RT · ln( A · t₁/₂ / ln 2 )
Eyring:  ΔH‡ = RT · [ ln(k_B T / h) + ΔS‡ / R + ln( t₁/₂ / ln 2 ) ]
For homolytic bond cleavage, radical recombination is barrierless ⇒ Eₐ ≈ ΔH‡ ≈ BDE.
Constants
R = 1.987 cal·mol⁻¹·K⁻¹   ·   k_B = 1.381×10⁻²³ J·K⁻¹   ·   h = 6.626×10⁻³⁴ J·s   ·   T(K) = T(°C) + 273.15
When to prefer which
• Arrhenius is fine when you have an empirical A from kinetic data (e.g. published initiator decomposition tables).
• Eyring is preferred when you want to reason about the transition state — separating ΔH‡ (≈ BDE for homolysis) from ΔS‡ (geometry/looseness of TS). Concerted/cyclic TSs have negative ΔS‡; loose homolytic TSs have positive ΔS‡.
• Caveat: Eₐ ≈ BDE only for clean unimolecular homolysis. Polar pathways, cage recombination, and concerted mechanisms break this approximation.
